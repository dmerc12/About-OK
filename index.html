Oklahoma

The Sooner State

"Labor Omnia Vincit" or "Work Conquers All Things"

Oklahoma is a state in the southern central plains of the United States bordering Kansas, Colorado, New Mexico, Texas, Arkansas, and Missouri. It was admitted statehood in 1907 when the Indian and Oklahoma Territories merged. Some cities in Oklahoma include Oklahoma City (or OKC), Tulsa, and Norman. The capital of Oklahoma City is home to the NBA team the Oklahoma City Thunder, which helped in earning the nickname of Loud City. Tulsa is home to many oil fields, which played a significant part in the development of the nickname, Oil Capital of the World. Finally, Norman is home to the the University of Oklahoma, which is where it gets it's nickname Home of the Sooners.

Oklahoma City

Oklahoma City is the largest city in the state. Its known for its cowboy culture and capitol complex.

Tulsa

Tulsa is the second-largest city in the state. Its known for its art deco architecture and has a rich history in the oil industry.

Norman

Norman is home to the University of Oklahoma, a major public research university. Its known for its vibrant college town atmosphere.
